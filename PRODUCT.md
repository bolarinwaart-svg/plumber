# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners and small commercial property managers in Leicester, UK, looking for a
plumbing and heating company they can trust. They often arrive mid-problem (a leak,
a broken boiler, a cold radiator) or while planning an installation. Context is
mixed: some are calm and comparing options, some are stressed and want to call now.
The job to be done: quickly judge whether Heat Wave Plumbing & Heating is competent,
local, and reachable, then make contact.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Heat Wave Plumbing & Heating. It exists to convert visitors into
enquiries by communicating range of services (plumbing, heating, installation,
emergency), local credibility, and an easy path to contact. Success is a visitor who
feels confident enough to call or fill the contact form.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy and established, modern and professional. Calm confidence: the work of
skilled local tradespeople presented with a contemporary, premium polish that sets
it apart from the typical dated plumber website. Voice is plain, reassuring, and
direct, never salesy or jargon-heavy.

## Anti-references

- Generic template plumber sites: stock-photo collages, garish blue gradients,
  clip-art tool icons, busy layouts.
- Cheap or dated: low-budget, cluttered, stuck-in-2010 aesthetics.
- Cold or corporate: sterile enterprise sterility with no human warmth. The site is
  professional but should still feel like real, approachable people.

## Design Principles

1. **Competence is shown, not claimed.** Restraint, alignment, and consistency
   signal a company that does careful work. Sloppy spacing reads as sloppy plumbing.
2. **Reassure under stress.** Many visitors arrive with a problem. Clear hierarchy,
   obvious contact paths, and calm pacing reduce friction.
3. **Local and human, not faceless.** Real imagery and warm, plain language over
   stock gloss and corporate abstraction.
4. **Premium through precision.** The "modern" feel comes from typographic
   discipline, deliberate whitespace, and a tight palette, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Sufficient text contrast (some visitors are older homeowners),
visible focus states, 44px minimum touch targets, and respect for
`prefers-reduced-motion`.
